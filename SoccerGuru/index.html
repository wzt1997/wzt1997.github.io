Project Info

Background

In 2020, we not only cheered for Bayern Munich’s winning the European Champian’s League but mourned for the unexpected passing away of Maradona. In 2020, we got accustomed to watching ‘silent’ soccer matches with no audience in the stadium. Last year, many tournaments and soccer leagues were postponed due to the epidemic. However, people’s passion for the sport does not fade at all.

As the acknowledged most popular sport in the world, soccer has long been playing a significant role in people’s life. Fans and journalists are delighted in asking interesting questions like who will be the best scorer in the Premier League? Which team will Messi transfer to? There are still many colorful and interesting stories to be dug from the statistics. To offer a better insight into the sport, we intend to resort to visualization tools to unmask some intriguing discoveries in the soccer world

Datasets

We decided to analyze FIFA player stats as well as the transfer data and found several helpful datasets on Kaggle and Github. We will mainly focus on the following stats:

Dataset List:

  • FIFA17 to FIFA21 statistics for each football player

    This dataset contains personal info, overall and detailed ratings of more than 17k players in the video game FIFA, from FIFA17 to FIFA21.

  • European football player transfers

    This dataset contains information of football player transfers from 1992/93 to 2020/21 seasons in 9 major football leagues.

  • Players stats per season

    This dataset contains statistics of players in recent seasons from 100+ tournaments.

  • Stadiums with GPS coordinates

    This dataset contains geographic info for 137 stadiums of different football teams currently. For this project, we apply the data from five major leagues, indicating the location data in Premier League, Ligue 1, 1 Bundesliga, Serie A and Primera Division.

Data Visualization

Visualization

Player Personal Ability Visualization

Inroduction:

This visualization is designed to show the statistics based on personal capabilities of the soccer players in 2019. The data is provided by the dataset 'FIFA17 to FIFA21 statistics for each football player'. This visualization is implemented by D3 and can be devided for mainly three parts:

  • Firstly, a filter is provided, with which users can select the player they are interested in.
  • For the selected player, we display his picture as well as other basic information. And a radar chart will be generated based on the player's capabilities of six main aspects: strength, speed, passing, shooting, dribbling and defense. In addition, we also depict the average level of all players in the radar chart for comparison. All the statistics are calculated based on the dataset.
  • At last, we show the detailed statistics of each ability of the player below, which are categorized into 7 more general classes.


Player and Team Statistics Visualization

Inroduction:

This visualization is designed to show the statistics based on the performance of players in different tournaments from 2017 to 2020. The data is provided by the dataset 'Players stats per season'. This visualization is also implemented by D3 and can be devided for mainly three parts:

  • Firstly, a filter is provided, with which users can select the player they are interested in.
  • For the selected player, we use a side-by-side bar chart comebined a line chart to illustrate the performance of a player throught out the years. If the player has taken taken part in more than one tournament, there will be more than one bar mark for each year and legends should be generated automatically. Apart from the bars, we also draw lines to show the change of performance of the players. What's more, you can select the field of data that you are interested in for visualisation, such as number of appearances, player raing and etc.
  • Besides, for the player's performance in a specific year, we will draw several pie chart to illustrate the player performance in more detail. We are interested in five aspects for pie charts depiction: Shots, GoalPostions, GoalMethods, Passes and DuelWon.


Transfer Visualization in Network

Inroduction:

We use a network graph to illustrate the transfer relationship between different soccer teams in the top-5 European soccer leagues. Each node in the network graph represents a team and each link represents the transfers in the selected year between the source node and the target node. Users can choose the year to get the view of European transfer market in different years.


Player Value Comparison

Inroduction:

Chasing bar diagrams are adopted to demonstrate the value of the players, on the basis of their transfer fee. Each bar in the diagram represents the transfer fee of the player and the diagram shows the top 10 most valuable players in the year. The data is provided by the dataset 'European football player transfers' dataset. This visualization is implemented by D3.js and can be divided for mainly two parts:

  • The Chasing Bar: The bar chart is designed to show the top 10 most valuable players each year. Through animation, the diagram is updated at the annual rate. Each bar in the diagram represents one player and on the right of the bar, the total transfer fee will be demonstrated.
  • The Line Chart: The line chart is designed to show the trend of the changes of the player through his career. The interactivity here is that when we click on the label or the bar of the player in the bar chart, the line chart will demonstrate the trend of the changes of past transfer fee from 1993 to 2020.


Team Transfer Demonstration

Inroduction:

Sankey Diagram is adopted to demonstrate the transfer history between different teams in the selected year. A Sankey Diagram is a visualisation technique that allows to display flows. Several entities (nodes) are represented by rectangles or text. Their links are represented with arrows or arcs that have a width proportional to the importance of the flow. The data is provided by the dataset 'European football player transfers' dataset. This visualization is implemented by D3.js and can be divided for mainly two parts:

  • The Filter: there are two filters in the sankey diagram, the first one is to choose the team to demonstrate the transfer information and the second one is to select the year.
  • The Sankey Map: The sankey map is designed for the transfer history between different teams in the selected year. The entity in the left part of the map is the selected team and in the right part, there exists several teams that have transactions with the selected team in the selected year. The interactivity here is that when we hover on the line of the sankey map, it will show the exact number of the transfer players at that year.


Player Transfer History

Inroduction:

Transfer History Map is adopted to demonstrate the transfer history of the selected player in his career. Each point on the map represents the location of a club and the link between them represents that the player has been transferred from the source club to the target one. The data is provided by the dataset 'European football player transfers' and ' Stadiums with GPS coordinates'. This visualization is implemented by echarts.js and can be divided for mainly two parts:

  • The Filter: the filter in the Transfer History Map is to choose the player to demonstrate the transfer history over the years.
  • The Transfer History Map: The transfer history map is designed for the transfer history of the selected player between different teams in his career. After we select the player in the filter, the map will show all the relevant clubs and connect them based on the transfer history. The animation demonstrates the transaction direction between the two clubs.
